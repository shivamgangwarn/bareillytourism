Temples of Bareilly

Alakhnath Temple

Alakhnath Temple is one of the most revered and ancient temples located in Bareilly, Uttar Pradesh. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is the main center of the Anand Akhara sect of Naga sadhus. This temple is not only a place of worship but also a center for spiritual learning and ascetic practices. The temple complex is expansive and unique, often characterized by its spiritual ambiance and the presence of ascetics who live and meditate here. The name “Alakhnath” refers to “Alakh Niranjan,” a term used to describe the formless and unmanifest aspect of God, which is worshipped by the Nath sect. Inside the temple, one can witness a blend of ancient and rustic architecture with a traditional design that includes courtyards, shrines, and residential areas for saints. The main sanctum houses a Shivling and other deities like Hanuman, Kali, and Bhairav. What makes this temple especially distinctive is the presence of various animals such as cows, peacocks, and even snakes, which freely roam within the temple premises – symbolizing a deep connection with nature. Alakhnath Temple becomes a hub of devotion during Shivratri and other Hindu festivals, attracting thousands of devotees from across India. The rituals, chants, and spiritual atmosphere during these times provide an immersive and divine experience. It is not just a temple but a living ashram where devotees and saints coexist, offering a rare glimpse into India’s rich spiritual heritage. Visitors often come here to meditate, seek blessings, or simply experience the calm and spiritually uplifting environment. Located in the heart of Bareilly, the Alakhnath Temple stands as a symbol of devotion, tradition, and the enduring legacy of the Nath Yogis. It is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the religious and cultural roots of Northern India.

Trivati Nath Temple

Trivati Nath Temple is one of the most ancient and revered temples in Bareilly, Uttar Pradesh. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple holds immense religious and cultural significance for the devotees of Shaivism. It is considered one of the “Nath” temples that form an important part of the city's spiritual identity. The name “Trivati Nath” is believed to have originated from three large banyan trees ("Trivati") under which the Shivling was originally discovered. According to legend, the temple was established centuries ago when Lord Shiva appeared in a dream to a local devotee, prompting the construction of the shrine at this sacred spot. Over time, the temple evolved into a significant pilgrimage site. The temple’s architecture reflects a traditional Hindu style, featuring a tall spire (shikhara), intricately carved walls, and a sanctum that houses the Shivling. The spiritual aura of the temple is amplified by the sound of bells, chanting of mantras, and the fragrance of incense sticks offered by devotees. Trivati Nath Temple becomes a focal point of devotion during festivals like Maha Shivratri, Shravan month, and Nag Panchami, drawing thousands of pilgrims from all over the region. The temple premises are beautifully decorated during these times, and special aartis and abhishekam rituals are performed. Apart from being a religious center, the temple also contributes to social and cultural activities in the city. It runs charitable services, including free food distribution and healthcare camps for the needy. Located near the heart of Bareilly, the Trivati Nath Temple is not just a site of worship, but a symbol of the city’s rich spiritual heritage. Visitors come here not only to offer prayers but also to find peace, devotion, and a deep sense of connection with the divine.

Dhopeshwar Nath Temple

Dhopeshwar Nath Temple is one of the most sacred and ancient temples in Bareilly, Uttar Pradesh. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this revered shrine holds deep mythological and religious significance, and is believed to be associated with the Pandavas from the Mahabharata era. It is said that the temple was established by Bhim, one of the five Pandava brothers, making it an important site of pilgrimage for devotees of Lord Shiva. Situated in the Cantonment area of Bareilly, Dhopeshwar Nath Temple is renowned for its peaceful surroundings and spiritually charged atmosphere. The temple houses a Shivling, which is believed to have self-emerged (Swayambhu), and devotees believe that offering prayers here fulfills their wishes and brings inner peace. The architecture of the temple is simple yet spiritually uplifting, reflecting the essence of traditional Indian temple design. The sanctum is the focal point where the Shivling resides, and daily rituals such as abhishekam (ceremonial bathing), aarti (devotional songs), and offering of bilva leaves are performed with devotion. Dhopeshwar Nath Temple draws thousands of devotees, especially during major Hindu festivals like Maha Shivratri and Shravan month. On these occasions, the temple is beautifully decorated with flowers and lights, and special pujas and community feasts are organized for the pilgrims. In addition to its religious importance, the temple also serves as a center for cultural and charitable activities. Free langars (meals), health check-up camps, and spiritual discourses are often held here, reflecting the temple's role in promoting social welfare and spiritual growth. Dhopeshwar Nath Temple stands as a symbol of Bareilly’s spiritual legacy, offering a tranquil and divine environment for prayer, meditation, and self-reflection. It is a must-visit for anyone seeking blessings, peace, and a deeper connection with Lord Shiva.
